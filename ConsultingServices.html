Blockchain

A blockchain is an ever-increasing list of records, called blocks.

These blocks are linked using cryptography.

Each block has a cryptographic hash of the previous block, a transaction, and timestamp.

Blockchain is the technology the underpins digital currencies such as Bitcoin, Ethereum and Ripple.

Blockchain allows digital info to be distributed, but does not allow it to be copied.

Each piece of data can only be in the possession of one person.

Autonomous Things

This is a new term for the technological developments that are expected to bring computers into the physical environment that are without the need for human direction.

They will be freely-moving and capable of interacting with humans and other objects.

An example of an autonomous thing is the SGR-A1 sentry gun that was deployed to help South Korean troops protect the Korean Demilitarised Zone.

It is considered the first unit of its kind to have an integrated system that has voice recognition, surveillance, tracking, and firing.

An everyday example would be a vacuum cleaner that can navigate around a house autonomously to clean floors.

Immersive Experience

Immersion into virtual reality is a perception of being present, physically, in a virtual world.

The perception is created by surrounding the user of the VR system by stimuli that create a convincing environment.

VR is most commonly used in entertainment applications such as video gaming and 3D cinema.
